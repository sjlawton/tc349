Info

MML is short for "Music Macro Language", a syntax used for encoding music in simple strings. These strings can then be used to play music in games, on phones, or for creating chiptunes to be played on older game consoles. They can also be converted to and from MIDI files using software.

While many of these devices implement MML slightly differently, for the most part they all follow similar rules. The letters a-g represent notes, + or - represent flats and sharps, r represents a rest, < and > change the octave, and t changes the tempo

For more information on MML files, visit Wikipedia
